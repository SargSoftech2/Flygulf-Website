OET WRITING GUIDE

OET Writing Grammar: Connectors

Feb 20, 2026 • 10 min read

Connectors are essential for achieving a high score in the "Cohesion and Coherence" criterion. However, overusing them can reduce the professional tone of your letter.

Recommended Connectors

  • Simple: and, but, or
  • Complex: however, therefore, although, unless, despite, in spite of, subsequently, consequently, owing to, in view of

Avoid in OET Writing

Ideal number of connectors in an OET letter is 2 or 3 maximum. If a sentence makes perfect sense without a connector, try not to use it.

Time Phrases

Use these to establish a clear chronological sequence in your letter:

On admission Upon discharge 2 days ago On his first visit During her hospital stay Today

Effective Use Examples

  • After a week in hospital, he has stabilised and his breathing problems are now resolved. However, he still experiences some chest and abdominal pain, with a dry cough.
  • Mr Ramamurthy was very weak on admission to hospital, but has gained weight with dietitian input.
  • Although she has a number of family members living nearby, their support is irregular, and Ms Martin may be at risk of social isolation.
  • Owing to her extreme dependence, she will require a home care nurse.
  • Despite good improvements, ongoing physiotherapy is recommended.
  • She presented with severe abdominal pain and subsequently underwent various investigations.
  • Ms Bell retired from a managerial position at the end of 2016, and has not found it easy to establish a new routine in her retirement.
  • She also complaints of polyuria and polydipsia; therefore, a possibility of diabetes needs to be ruled out.
  • Since October 2018, Mr Dunbar has shown signs of diabetic neuropathy and consequently mobilises with a walking stick.
  • In spite of regular exercise, she has developed many lifestyle diseases

    Confusing Connectors

    As well as vs. And:

    "She needs aspirin as well as ramipril" implies ramipril is less important.

  • She needs aspirin and ramipril- both are equally important.

    • 2.hence

    The use of hence in the beginning of a sentence is not recommended.

    3.Despite:

    Never use a subject after 'despite'. Correct: "Despite improvements..." (Noun). Incorrect: "Example: Despite he has improved, ongoing care is recommended. Despite improvements, ongoing care is recommended."
